FAQ (Frequently Asked Questions)

Who is HostWebflow Best For?

Most Small Business Websites Receive Less Than 500 Visits Per Month. The regular Webflow plans prices are structured for larger-scale websites that can handle upwards of 30,000 visits per month.

Host Webflow is best for individuals/businesses that want to pay for hosting services that fit their specific website goals. If your Website falls between 0-20,000 visits per month, hostwf is your Pick. If you have >20,000 visits, you can contact us and we will add more bandwidth.

What are "AutoExports"?

Auto Exports are essentially us handling your Webflow exports. This is useful when you don't want to pay for any Webflow plan and also want to export a Webflow website to HostWebflow. If you run out of Auto Exports you can manually import at any point in time as much as possible.

What is Bandwidth?

Website bandwidth refers to the volume of data from a website that can be transmitted from its server to a user's device.

To compute bandwidth: Suppose your homepage has a size of 1.5 MB, and it receives 10,000 visits. The bandwidth consumed would be 1.5 MB multiplied by 10,000, equating to approximately 15,000 MB or 15GB.

When faced with bandwidth constraints, it can be severely beneficial to optimize your website's content, especially images, and videos, to ensure efficient data transfer and faster loading times.

What if i get an unexpected rise in website traffic beyond my allocated bandwidth?

No worries! Our servers are equipped to handle sudden increases in your website traffic. If this surge in visitors is a one-time event, rest assured, you won't incur any additional charges.

However, if this trend continues over several months, we'd advise considering an upgrade to enhance your bandwidth allocation or shifting to a standard Webflow plan.

An added perk for those on our multi-site plan: bandwidth is flexible across your sites. So, if one website is consuming more bandwidth compared to another, it can tap into the unused bandwidth from the lesser-visited sites. This ensures optimal usage of resources across your group of websites

What are some limitations?

There are "2 Major Limitations", The first is Webflow forms, Webflow forms only work when your website is hosted on Webflow. We suggest using forms like Tally forms or Formly when requesting user submissions. Other than that, we circumvent any limitation hosting off the Webflow platform poses (at least for small to medium-sized websites)

The second is unfortunately CMS, due to the nature of how Webflow exports its sites, any CMS section/page has to be converted to static manually. :(
